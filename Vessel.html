Vessel

To be a vessel is to hold -- to lend space. To be a vessel is to be defined by the potential of emptiness.

"We mold clay into a pot,
but it is the emptiness inside
that makes the vessel useful.

We fashion wood for a house,
but it is the emptiness inside
that makes it livable.

We work with the substantial,
but the emptiness is what we use."

In this project, we explore what it means to create a vessel, and in doing so, to create emptiness -- void, nothingness, lack, potential. - The Tao Te Ching, by Lao Tzu. Translated by John H. McDonald.
